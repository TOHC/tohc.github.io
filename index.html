DataHacks 2016

Location: Northern Secondary School

Date: Janurary 31, 2016

Time: 10:30 AM - 4:30 PM

Every two days now we create as much information as we did from the dawn of civilization up until 2003, according to Google CEO Eric Schmidt. Data science is an up-and-coming discipline that's all about analyzing and bringing structure to large amounts of incoherent data. The demand for data scientists is increasing rapidly. It is predicted that by 2018, there will be a 50 percent gap in the supply of data scientists versus demand. DataHacks is an event devoted to the wonders of data science and artificial intelligence. Students will get the opportunity to learn advanced concepts in the fields of data mining and machine learning all for free! The event will consist of snacks, numerous hands-on workshops, and a team competition. There are no prerequisites and beginners are welcome!

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Toronto Hacker Club?

We are a student-run organization aspiring to popularize computer science throughout high schools in the GTA.


How much experience do I need to participate in events?

None! We have events targeting people of all different skill levels.


What is the cost of attending an event?

Admission fees vary from event to event. Many introductory events are free of charge.


Is it worth it to learn computer programming?

Absolutely! Computer programming is one of the few skills that are in demand and is an invaluable experience to one's future, regardless of major or career choice.
